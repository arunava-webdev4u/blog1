And talking about microprocessors, how can we talk about microprocessors without saying the name Intel, the face of the industry. Intel Corporation is an American multinational corporation and technology headquartered in Santa Clara, California, in Silicon Valley. It is the world's largest and highest valued semiconductor chip. Intel’s early history is legendary. Intel was founded on July 18, 1968, by semiconductor pioneers Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who had left Fairchild Semiconductor. Originally called “NM Electronics” — for Noyce and Moore — the company purchased the right to use the name “Intel,” short for Integrated Electronics, from a company called Intelco.

Intel paid $60,000 to buy the rights to its first core chip from Japanese calculator company Busicom, which was reportedly strapped for cash at the time. Intel with the help of Ted Hoff launched the world's first microprocessor, named 4004, a 4-bit single-core 1/8-inch by 1/6-inch chip, on November 15, 1971, and it served as the foundation for Intel’s microprocessor-based computing. This “computer on a chip” had 2,300 MOS transistors that performed 60,000 OPS (operations per second) and cost $200.

  • Then Intel introduced the 8008 processor(200-800 kHz) on April 1, 1972. Intel’s first 8-bit (single-core) processor andit was twice powerful than 4004.

  • Intel’s improved microprocessor chip was introduced on April 1, 1974; the 8080 (2 MHz,8-bit single-core) became a standard in the computer industry. In 1980, the 4004 was followed by the 8080, which was chosen as the central processing unit of IBM’s first personal computer.

  • After that Intel introduced the 8085 processor in March 1976, Intel 8086(5 Mhz) on June 8, 1976, it was the first 16-bit processor, it gave rise to the x86 architecture, which eventually became intel’s most successful line of processors.

  • On June 1, 1979, intel launched a cheaper version of 8086 that was intel 8088(5Mhz).

  • The Motorola 68000, a 16/32-bit processor was released and was later chosen as the processor for the Apple Macintosh andAmiga computers in 1979.

  • In 1982 intel 286 was introduced.

  • Intel 386 (16 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1985. It has 275,000 transistors.

  • Intel 486 (25 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1989. It has 1.2 million transistors.

  • Intel Pentium (66 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1993. It has 3.1 million transistors.

  • Cyrix released the Cx5x86 processor in 1995, in an attempt to compete with the Intel Pentium processors.

  • Intel Pentium Pro (200 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1995. It has 5.5 million transistors.

  • Intel Pentium II (300 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1997. It has 7.5 million transistors.

  • Intel Celeron (266 Mhz,32-bit) was introduced in 1998. It has 7.5 million transistors.

  • Intel Pentium III (1.0 GHz,32-bit) was introduced in 1999. It has 9.5 million transistors.

  • Intel Pentium 4 (1.4 GHz,32-bit) was introduced in 2000. It has 42 million transistors. From this intel introduced LGA sockets around 2004 for easy use.

  • Intel Xeon (1.7 GHz,32-bit) was introduced in 2001. It has 55 million transistors. Targetted exclusively at the non-consumer, server and embedded system market.

  • Intel Pentium M (1.7 GHz,32-bit) was introduced in 2003. It has 55 million transistors.

  • Intel Core Solo & Duo (1.06 to 2.33 GHz,32-bit) was introduced in 2006. It has 151 million transistors. It was intel’s first dual-core mobile processor.

  • Intel Core 2 (2.66 GHz,32-bit,1,2 or 4-core) was introduced in 2006. It has 291 million transistors. It was the first set of processors based on core microarchitecture.

  • Intel Atom (1.86 GHz,32-bit,1 to 8-cores) was introduced in 2008. It has 47 million transistors. It was low power, low cost and low-performance microprocessor that mainly used int notebooks and other devices.

  • Intel Core i7 (2.66 – 3.2 GHz,64-bit,4 to 10 cores) was introduced in 2008. It has 731 million transistors. It targeted high-end consumer markets for both laptop and desktop users. Intel first introduced powerful onboard graphics in this and maintained it later on.

  • Intel Core i5 (2.66 GHz,64-bit,2 to 4 cores) was introduced in 2009. It has 774 million transistors. It targeted the mainstream consumer market for both laptop and desktop users.

  • Intel Core i3 (2.93 – 3.07 GHz,64-bit,2- cores) was introduced in 2010. It has 382 million transistors. It was intel’s new entry-level processor for both laptop and desktop users.

  • Intel released its first processor to utilize the BGA-1364 socket and feature an Iris Pro Graphics 5200 GPU. Released in June 2013, it runs at 3.2 GHz.

  • Intel released the first Core i9 desktop processor, the i9-7900X, in June 2017. It uses the LGA 2066 socket, runs at 3.3 GHz, has 10 cores.

  • Intel released the first Core i9 mobile processor, the i9-8950HK, in April 2018. It uses the BGA 1440 socket, runs at 2.9 GHz, has six cores.

  • This was a summary of processors launched by this giant brand. At today's date, intel already introduced the 10th gen processor.
